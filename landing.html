Common Questions

Everything you need to know about Family Flow

What about concerns with excessive screen time affecting child development?

These concerns are valid and important. Research shows excessive passive screen consumption can impact social development, particularly eye contact and face-to-face interaction skills.

How Family Flow specifically counters these concerns:

  • Active family interaction: App requires looking at each other and talking, not passive consumption
  • Brief exposure: 2-minute daily sessions minimize screen time while maximizing connection
  • Face-to-face focus: Prompts discussions that happen offline, away from screens
  • Eye contact practice: Mood sharing and emotional check-ins encourage direct eye contact
  • Graduation mindset: Explicitly designed to move families away from digital dependency

Important: Family Flow works best as a catalyst for connection, not a replacement. If your family already connects well naturally, that's ideal—no app needed.

How is this different from parental control apps?

Parental controls impose external limits. Family Flow builds internal awareness. Instead of "You can't," we ask "How did that feel?" and "What do you want to try next?"

We don't restrict screen time or block apps. Instead, we help families reflect on their digital choices and develop awareness together. You decide what works for your family.

Is my family's data really private?

Absolutely. Nothing you enter leaves your device—ever. There are no accounts, no surveillance, no cloud sync, and you can delete everything with one tap.

Even the export features only process data locally in your browser, not sent anywhere. Your family's journey stays with your family.

Why doesn't Family Flow use AI features?

AI creates dependency, not independence. While other apps use AI for personalized responses, Family Flow deliberately avoids this to protect authentic family connections.

Why we stay human-centered:

  • Intimacy matters: AI presence changes the nature of vulnerable family conversations
  • Graduation conflicts: AI features encourage ongoing app dependency instead of family independence
  • Authentic growth: Families develop better emotional intelligence by trusting their own insights
  • Privacy protection: No AI analysis means your intimate family moments stay completely private
